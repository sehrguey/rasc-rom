My part in the planned project was executed on all the points, which stripped my further living on of any goal whatsoever. Life still rolled along the rutted track, yet aimlessly already and unplanned. However, if you don't ask the useless question "what for?", then a Thursday with the bathhouse, and the steam room, and the subsequent two bottles of beer would suffice to motivate living for another week. In my, not quite Tibetan, yet well-structured way of life there felt an undeniable lack of carnal pleasures. I caught myself thinking this thought on the evening when, coming after work to Decemberists 13, I cast the usual glance at the mingle-mangle crowd of shoes and slippers about the shoe shelf on the veranda. The in-depth self-investigation that followed the hot trail of the glance, made it clear that my eyes were trying to make out the high-wedge Austrian high boots there. Of course, it was not the eyes' fault that made in Austria footwear was so durable and unwilling to wear out from my memory. Yet, what high boots might possibly come to the veranda in summer? Turning a couple of times to my cautious steps over the expanded clay—I neared the doorway to make the details of the supposed masterpiece more precise—the model asked Trepetilikha, who was plastering a jamb in the same room, "Could that bozo stole my trowel?"

"Not a chance," replied Trepetilikha. "This one if even stumbles on your trowel would never pilfer it."

But you cannot lace up a blizzard... Good news, it's not whipping my face, but pushing from behind towards the station in the early morning twilight. Dense streams of snow in the squally wind push the twilight back towards the darkness.

Knee-deep in snowdrifts I flounder on by the supposed service path alongside the railway track. The concrete pillars holding the contact wire above the rails serve the milestones not to get lost in the desert of floating snow.
